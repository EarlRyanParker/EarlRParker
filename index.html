Microprocessor Systems Project:

Spatial Mapping Using Time-of-Flight

Winter 2022

Commerical LIDAR equipment can be expensive and bulky. the goal of this project is to mimic LIDAR application in a manner that is less expensive, compact, and suitable for indoor exploration. In this project we design and build an embedded spatial measurement system using a time-of-flight sensor to acquire information about the area around you. Using a rotary mechanism to provide a 360 degree measurement of distance within a single vertical geometric plane (e.g., y-z), you must integrate fixed distance samples along the orthogonal axis (e.g., x-axis). Mapped spatial information is stored in onboard memory and later communicated to a personal computer or web application for reconstruction and graphical presentation.

Integrated Design Projects:

Source Water Monitoring System

Winter 2022

Climate change is significantly impacting the quality of our drinking water sources through floods, wildfires, and harmful algal blooms. One of the biggest challenges faced by drinking water treatment plants is sudden changes to source water quality. When this happens, the plant must quickly adapt its treatment process. As such, early detection of changes in source water quality is critical in the protection of human health. The goal of this project is to design a system using drones, image analysis, and machine learning to monitor source waters for algal blooms. The design must consider errors (i.e., detection of algae where there is none, or vice versa), and make technical decisions to reduce errors (e.g., fly height, image quality, number and frequency of images, weather conditions). Flight paths should also be considered.
